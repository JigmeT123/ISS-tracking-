Facts About ISS

1. The station travels from west to east on an orbital inclination of 51.6 degrees. Each orbit takes 90-93 minutes, depending on the exact altitude of the ISS.

2. With each orbit taking 90-93 minutes, there are approximately 16 orbits per day (24 hours).

3. There is an approximate repeat of orbit tracks over the same area on the ground every 3 days. Again, the ISS altitude will determine how closely the tracks repeat.
